CREATING A FLOCKING SYSTEM
WITH JAVASCRIPT

Flocking behaviour is the reason birds fly together the way starlings do at dusk, and how they seem to know what direction to turn in without hitting each other.

A Flocking System (a group following flocking behaviour) can also be created using three dimensional computational geometry by applying 3 behaviours to an object/particle. It is important that I don’t go any further without referencing Craig Reynolds, the man who discovered this equation and created the first digital flocking system.

Simulated boid flock avoiding cylindrical obstacles (1986) by Craig Reynolds



A real bird can fly the way it does because it has very limited vision. It can only see in, let’s say for arguments sake, a 1 meter radius. This does not hinder the bird’s sight, but rather allows fly better because it only has to concentrate on the direction & speed of those surrounding it, whilst not bumping into them.

When every bird is concentrating on these three things, it creates the beautiful flocks you see in the sky at dusk.



o when creating this in Javascript, you take a vector moving from point A to point B and apply these same rules. To get this ‘Boid’ (Craig Reynolds coined this term - it’s a portmanteau of bird and droid in case you didn’t catch on) to make sure it’s going in the right direction, you get it to calculate the average direction of the other boids within it’s ‘1 meter’ radius; and then move in that direction. This is called alignment.

A similar equation is done to not bump into others (this is called separation), and to seek out a nearby flock if flying solo or in a small group (this is called cohesion). These equations then add a steering force to the boid, getting it to move smoothly and in a flock.
